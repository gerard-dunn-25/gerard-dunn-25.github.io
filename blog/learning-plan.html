Bootcamp Learning Plan:

9 February 2025

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goals are to build upon the knowledge I gain during the Dev Academy course and pave a pathway to a job in the web development industry as I believe this is a career that I can be enthusiastic about which is a significant change from where I was in my past jobs. Until recently, I had been working full time in a job that was enough to pay the bills but didn't challenge me or develop my professional skills in any meaningful way so I see this opportunity as a chance to accomplish both of those outcomes at the same time.

While I'm still studying, I'd love to build upon the foundations that these last 5 weeks have provided me while enjoying the learning process. I expect that Bootcamp will be a pretty significant change from foundations, so I want to make sure to stay focused but still enjoy myself while we delve deeper into the key parts of the course.

2. What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strengths will hopefully be my interpersonal skills, as I would love to build stronger relationships with the people I have met during foundations. Interacting in person will be a new dynamic and I think it will be a better opportunity to work with other students and network with people who will likely end up working in the same field as me.

I feel like I have a pretty competent grasp on the concepts that we have covered during foundations so I'm hoping I will be able to hone my skills and draw on this knowledge during Bootcamp. Being able to ask people for help or guidance in person will also be a more effective way to reinforce a lot of this knowledge as interacting through discord has been very convenient but I feel like face to face interactions will be more memorable.

My limitations will still be based on how reluctant I am to ask for help, as I will likely still want to solve things for myself at first. This is something I have definitely gotten better at over the course of Foundations but I will certainly need to identify how long is too long to be stuck on any one problem given the accelerated pace of Bootcamp.

3. What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I am expecting that we will have to do group or paired tasks like the pair programming so the biggest challenge may be related to a difference of opinion between myself and other students. I don't see this as being too much of a problem as we are all learning and healthy debate over how to complete a task will result in us researching a topic more thoroughly. I certainly don't want to insult or upset anyone though, so being kind and thoughtful when having these types of conversations will be important to bear in mind as we all hold different perspectives.

I also want to manage my time well and make sure that I am submitting all my work within the expected timeframes. I have been pretty good at this during foundations but I don't want my standards to slip if the material we are working with becomes more complex.

4. What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

My reflective skills could likely be an area that I would like to work on as I want to be thinking meaningfully about the work we are doing in order to let it resonate and stick in my long term memory. This is an experience that I am choosing to undergo as someone in their 30's, so I want anything I do during the course to be considered and have an impact on the learning experience I go through.

Not that this is something that I have actively put myself through in the past, but my partner and people at my previous job, often talked about my leadership potential. This is certainly something that I could foster and develop during the Bootcamp portion of the course if the opportunity presents itself.

Foundation Learning Plan:

16 January 2025

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I would like to develop my skills in this area to the point where I am a competent web developer, able to look for work in a position that provides me growth opportunities and the ability to turn my learnings into a long term career.

I understand that often means starting at the bottom as an entry level or junior developer and working your way up, but I am committed to doing so if that is what it takes.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strengths are in my interests and dedication to throw myself into whatever I am doing. I often find myself going down the rabbit hole as it were relating to things that I am interested in but I view this as a strength as I tend to come out of it learning more about that topic than if I only skimmed the surface. Learning how to dial that back to be at the level where I learn enough about a topic to move forward confidently but not overdoing it will be a key strength I will develop over my time at Dev Academy.

I have been doing computer based work for the better part of a decade at this stage so my computer literacy is already decent and will only get better the more I practice the lessons put in front of me.

My limitations are potentially in my attention span, this could be something that becomes evident if I ever miss a deadline or go over a time box for instance. As mentioned above, I tend to get really invested in particular topics so if I am not managing my time effectively and attempting to stick to the designated time given for a particular task, then I could see this being an issue.

These last two weeks have been a really good test for sticking to the time box of a given topic and I have been pretty successful at not overdoing it but this is something I will be trying to stay on top of.

3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to work on my collaborative skills, working as part of a team and working effectively with others to meet deadlines or objectives.

For a very long time in my professional life, I have been doing self directed and rather isolated work. In my previous job, I was the only person who did the particular function that my role served so there were little to no opportunities to work with others on the same project or piece of work. This was pretty isolating but I also developed skills relating to being self-directed and self-motivating as if I didn‚Äôt do that work, then it wouldn‚Äôt get done.

I would like to develop my soft skills more to be an effective communicator and team player so that I am prepared for entering a web developer role and the requirements that position would have in this regard.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I know that I am responsible for my own learning and managing my own workload is very important if I want to be successful in this course. I am committed to completing what is requested of me to the deadlines that I have been provided.

In order to do this effectively I will be very strict with limiting anything that could potentially distract me and during ‚Äúwork time‚Äù only focusing on the tasks that I have set for myself. This will also help me to develop my relationships during my studies and prepare me for the networking that will become a large part of professional life in this space.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I have been familiar with discord for a number of years now, thanks to my friends and I using it as a social hub for catching up virtually, sharing messages or memes and for voice chat while gaming.

This means that if I am struggling with any work in the course, I know that I will be able to seek help in the discord from the Facilitators or other students. I commit to utilising this resource effectively and when in need rather than struggle with a problem for a prolonged period of time.
