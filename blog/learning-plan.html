THS01 - Revised Learning Plan:

23 March 2025

Part I - Reflect

How accurate were your foundations expectations of your learning needs and growth areas?

I would say that my foundation expectations were pretty close to the actual reality of life during bootcamp but I simply didn't know how difficult some of the topics would be or how involved the human skills deep dives would be for the wider course. Looking back over my initial learning plan and my follow-up before bootcamp started, it's clear that I had an open mind and was willing to seek growth through learning and that has now become one of my main focuses when we look at our most important needs.

Were there any surprises / did you learn new things about yourself?

This whole course so far has been learning a lot of new things about myself but also reinforcing the things I already knew about my character, which represent who I am as a person. In my previous learning plan, I talked about wanting to focus on interpersonal and communication skills, which is something that I have definitely done during bootcamp. I have received positive encouragement around this in the form of other students vocalizing how much they enjoy working with me or like me as a person and also these same sentiments being expressed to me by both the technical and human skills facilitators .

What challenges/barriers did you find in following your learning and wellbeing plan? How might you overcome some of these barriers as you proceed through the rest of your time in bootcamp?

For the challenges or barriers that I have faced, it has primarily been about the physical wellbeing aspects which directly affect mental wellbeing. Sticking to a structured routine with a consistent bedtime has been a real challenge as I tend to struggle with sleep, but it often results in me have very poor rest and ending up being overly tired during class. This has not been conducive to effective learning and I've certainly had to revisit certain lectures if there are parts I think I've not fully engaged with. As we're getting to the business end of the course, these next few weeks are going to be super important to show up to feeling rested and ready to go, so I'm going to be enforcing a much stricter bedtime in order to combat this challenge.

What benefits did you experience from following your learning and wellbeing plan?

The primary benefit I found from following my learning plan was the feeling of being accountable to yourself. It is often the case that when you say you're going to do something or act in a certain way, unless it's in writing, things can be fluid and not adhered to without much risk of feeling like you're going back on your word. When it IS in writing however, you feel much more obliged to follow through on the things you said you would do or the ways you said you would act. I have found this to be very beneficial for me as I like to think that I am a pretty consistent person, but seeing that the things I wrote down in my learning plan are very much the ways that I have been conducting myself during this course has been reassuring and validating.

Bootcamp Learning Plan:

9 February 2025

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goals are to build upon the knowledge I gain during the Dev Academy course and pave a pathway to a job in the web development industry as I believe this is a career that I can be enthusiastic about which is a significant change from where I was in my past jobs. Until recently, I had been working full time in a job that was enough to pay the bills but didn't challenge me or develop my professional skills in any meaningful way so I see this opportunity as a chance to accomplish both of those outcomes at the same time.

While I'm still studying, I'd love to build upon the foundations that these last 5 weeks have provided me while enjoying the learning process. I expect that Bootcamp will be a pretty significant change from foundations, so I want to make sure to stay focused but still enjoy myself while we delve deeper into the key parts of the course.

2. What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strengths will hopefully be my interpersonal skills, as I would love to build stronger relationships with the people I have met during foundations. Interacting in person will be a new dynamic and I think it will be a better opportunity to work with other students and network with people who will likely end up working in the same field as me.

I feel like I have a pretty competent grasp on the concepts that we have covered during foundations so I'm hoping I will be able to hone my skills and draw on this knowledge during Bootcamp. Being able to ask people for help or guidance in person will also be a more effective way to reinforce a lot of this knowledge as interacting through discord has been very convenient but I feel like face to face interactions will be more memorable.

My limitations will still be based on how reluctant I am to ask for help, as I will likely still want to solve things for myself at first. This is something I have definitely gotten better at over the course of Foundations but I will certainly need to identify how long is too long to be stuck on any one problem given the accelerated pace of Bootcamp.

3. What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I am expecting that we will have to do group or paired tasks like the pair programming so the biggest challenge may be related to a difference of opinion between myself and other students. I don't see this as being too much of a problem as we are all learning and healthy debate over how to complete a task will result in us researching a topic more thoroughly. I certainly don't want to insult or upset anyone though, so being kind and thoughtful when having these types of conversations will be important to bear in mind as we all hold different perspectives.

I also want to manage my time well and make sure that I am submitting all my work within the expected timeframes. I have been pretty good at this during foundations but I don't want my standards to slip if the material we are working with becomes more complex.

4. What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

My reflective skills could likely be an area that I would like to work on as I want to be thinking meaningfully about the work we are doing in order to let it resonate and stick in my long term memory. This is an experience that I am choosing to undergo as someone in their 30's, so I want anything I do during the course to be considered and have an impact on the learning experience I go through.

Not that this is something that I have actively put myself through in the past, but my partner and people at my previous job, often talked about my leadership potential. This is certainly something that I could foster and develop during the Bootcamp portion of the course if the opportunity presents itself.

5. What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect that the Bootcamp team will be able to help us inspect our code and point us in the right direction if we need guidance. I am looking forward to getting to meet and know these people over the course of the next 9 weeks and that they will be open to answering questions we may have about the content we are learning as we go.

I expect that support will cover both technical and human skills learnings and will help us develop these skills in order to prepare us for a job in the industry. Support in this way may be pointing us in the direction of interesting or important article and resources.

6. What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will strive to reach out for assistance as soon as I am struggling with any content that I find difficult and will be in an environment where other students or staff are within arms reach, so to speak, so there should be no significant barrier.

I commit to working through the workload requirements to the best of my abilities in a timely fashion, this includes any projects that are group oriented and therefore hold myself accountable to the others I am working with. I promise to be respectful of those around me, both staff and students, and that I will do my best to be mindful of the shared spaces that we are all operating in.
