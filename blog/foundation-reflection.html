Foundation Reflection:

9 February 2025

◦ What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

Since starting this course, I have learnt a lot about how I approach problem solving and how I can often be a bit hard headed when faced with a difficult task. Asking for help is a key part of foundations and there are many methods we have been provided for reaching out when we struggle. This first example is something that I have gotten better at over the last 5 weeks but I still need to encourage myself to reach out when I hit a wall as there are plenty of people who can either help or may have experienced the same things.

I have learnt how to examine myself and my strengths in order to apply them to the tasks that we have been given. This has given me the opportunity to reflect on how I behave under pressure and in turn try different problem solving techniques to see what I relate to or find effective. The Tic Tac Toe and Whack-a-Mole tasks have been the best examples of tasks that I found difficult but persevered through and completed as a result.

Lastly, I have challenged myself to be strict and limit distractions during my studies. In the past, I have almost always had something going on in the background while I worked or relaxed as it acted almost as some form of white noise, but during the foundations part of the course, I have actively focused on limiting these distractions so that I can really absorb the information I'm trying to learn. Trying to unlearn these habits has been a challenge, but I feel as though I have benefited from having a focused mindset when it's needed most.

◦ What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

As most employers in the industry care about how you will fit into or affect the culture of their workplace, learning the value of soft skills has been an important lesson.

Values are an important part of your identity that show others what you consider important or meaningful. If your values align with the others you work with, it is far easier to get along with each other and build deeper relationships, which in turn makes it easier to work and collaborate together.

Empathy is a vital soft skill that shows you are able to consider how others are thinking or feeling. In a learning or programming environment, this could look like recognising when someone is struggling or needing help and offering your assistance during this time.

Self awareness is the ability to recognise your strengths or weaknesses and how that will affect the current or upcoming tasks you may be responsible for. This is the perfect opportunity to reach out and ask for help if you know you may need to learn the skills that are required for success of the task or offer your help if you believe you can lean into your strengths to provide your assistance.

◦ What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I suppose what surprised me most about the Human Skills concepts was just how much focus was put on these learnings during each sprint. I'm sure this may be a common viewpoint held by students starting this course, but I expected the programming side to take up the majority of the sprint, with some time spent on Human Skills but it feels like more of a 50 / 50 approach.

After spending some time with these concepts, I can definitely see the value that they hold and how they will be vital lessons to have learnt and be able to apply in a workplace.

◦ What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

I felt as though the hardest parts of the Human Skills learning was challenging any preconceived ideas I had around what these concepts meant or how they applied to me. Looking into my own strengths, identity and values, meant taking an honest look in the mirror and seeing where I could improve or learn more.

After working a full time job for nearly a decade and deciding to come back to study, I knew there would be a shift in mentality studying a new subject that I knew little to nothing about, but I feel as though learning or relearning some of these soft skills was an aspect of this course that I didn't fully anticipate.

◦ Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Learning to code is obviously a very big part of becoming a web developer, but if that's all there was to getting a job in this field, Dev Academy would likely focus more on that as the curriculum. It has been said already, but employers are not looking for the most efficient or technical programmer when they go through the hiring process, they are more interested in whether you'll be a good fit for their businesses culture and that means having the right social and soft skills.

Technical ability can be taught but social soft skills are not something they want to have to teach their employees, it's much more appealing for them to find candidates who already meet these ideals.

◦ Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

I think focusing on and learning how to cultivate these soft skills is a good method to break up the technical material in a way that is complimentary. If we were only ever learning the coding material, it would likely be overwhelming and would not provide the opportunity to sit with a topic for a while before moving onto the next technical skill.

I certainly don't feel like having the Human Skills portion of the course has been a waste, as I have still been given plenty of opportunities to learn and practice coding subjects. I also feel like the pace of each sprint has slowly ramped up over time while maintaining a good balance of technical and human skills. If the course was solely technical skills from the start, it would be much more difficult for new students who are completely fresh to a lot of these subjects, such as myself.
